A Split Entity

To me, there's something beautiful about unreciprocated love: A phrase tainted by society, a phrase that marks instability. It's become provocative in stature and establishes expectance, it dictates that to receive one must first give.

So it is natural, you see, to give a lot to others for me,
yet it's selfish, you see, when others aren't like me
and feel no need to go out of their way to present me.

So this is how I lived, for years on end; casting a line out to space and gambling for a slight nibble in its place. Constantly throwing my hands to the sky asking "why.." Only to turn and walk away with dried tears in my eyes.

That is until I found an entity orbiting my life. It's been there for years observing my lines with a head cocked to the side. "Just a little too far.." is what I'd say as I walk away only to try another day. This went on for weeks, months, years until a tear dropped from my nose and landed in the pond, rippling the mirrored surface and distorting all I believed.

It's only then that I looked up to the sky with my hands calmly placed by my side I tilted my head to the right at the same time my line caught a bite. I dropped my rod and it was pulled in the pond. Pondering the perfect way to respond: how to communicate I'm no longer lost.

Looking to the horizon, a smile crosses my face. The Moon is setting, but I know it'll return another day when I can once again feel it's embrace. Until then I keep my eyes level and step into new space.

Author's notes: I weave together parts of my past with my own inner dialogue to try and create imagery that demonstrates mental development.

In A Split Entity I take a look at my stance on love and realize it's not a give and receive thing like I had thought before. Instead, It's a measure of consistency and understanding. I draw heavily from times I've spent with a dear friend of mine and metaphorically relate the mental fracturing of me as a disillusioned fisherman to the dissolvement of my own internal longing for affection and love from those around me.
